Introduction
The Problem: In the fast-paced world of recruitment, HR professionals and hiring managers are drowning in paperwork. They spend countless hours crafting job descriptions (JDs) that are often ambiguous and manually screening hundreds of resumes (CVs), leading to fatigue, unconscious bias, and a slow hiring process.

The Solution: We built TalentScout AI, a ‚ÄúMini ATS‚Äù (Applicant Tracking System) powered by Google Cloud. TalentScout uses Gemini 2.0 Flash Experimental to act as an intelligent hiring manager. It performs two critical tasks in a single click:

Audits the Job Description for clarity and generates interview questions.
Auto-Ranks Candidates from the database by semantically comparing their skills against the JD in real-time.
Target Audience: HR Managers and Recruiters

Design
We designed this solution with three key principles in mind: Speed, Simplicity, and Scalability.

We chose a serverless architecture to handle the application logic and hosting. This means we don‚Äôt have to manage infrastructure; Google Cloud handles the scaling automatically, allowing us to focus entirely on the code and AI integration.

High-Level Architecture:
Rationale Behind the Choices:

Frontend/Backend Logic: Streamlit (Python): Streamlit allows us to build a beautiful, interactive web UI entirely in Python rapidly. It‚Äôs perfect for data and AI applications where speed-to-demo is crucial.
AI Engine: Vertex AI (Gemini 2.0 Flash Experimental): We specifically chose the gemini-2.0-flash-exp model. As a "Flash" model, it offers incredibly low latency, making the user experience feel instantaneous. Its advanced multimodal and multilingual capabilities allow it to understand complex JDs and CVs in various languages without extra configuration.
Deployment: Google Cloud Run: We chose this because it scales to zero (cost-effective) and handles the heavy lifting of AI processing without managing servers.
Database: Cloud Firestore: We use this NoSQL document database to store a history of analyzed jobs. Its flexible schema is perfect for storing the JSON-like structured output generated by the AI.
